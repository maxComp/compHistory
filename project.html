Computers assist engineering management decisions
Idaho Statesman May 30, 1976

At Morrison-Knudsen Co., computers assist engineering management decisions. A modeling system used for several years is "project control," which gives' managers some advantage of hindsight before a project is started, according to Steve McIntyre, manager of data processing.

"With this you can easily play the game of (what ifs..)" he said. For example, a particular construction job is programmed for normal conditions. Then managers may see what delays are incurred if, for example, a strike occurs, or if a shipment of equipment is delayed. "lt could take months to figure out some of these "what ifs" by hand, but with a computer you have the answer in five minutes," he said.
