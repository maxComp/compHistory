Special course added at BJC for Fall
Idaho Statesman September 1, 1964

Being offered for the first time is a course titled, Introduction to Electronic Data Processing. Scheduled for Wednesday nights at 7 p.m. The course will be business oriented and will deal mainly in the area of accounting functions. The course objectives are to increase the amount of information and knowledge of students in the subject of data processing programming and to improve the ability of persons working in the data processing field.

Ralph Hull, chief programmer of the Boise-Cascade Corporation, is the instructor. Students who plan to enroll should have the basic principles of data processing or some experience or knowledge of data processing. They will also be given a programmers aptitude test during the first class meeting.
