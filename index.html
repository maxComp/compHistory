The first Computers in Boise City

The history of computing in Boise began on June 30, 1957 when Remington Rand corporation demonstated a UNIVAC computer in their office at 609 Bannock Street. At this time the world had only 1,100 computer installations.

Later that year the Department of Highways bought a UNIVAC 120 from Remington Rand and put it to use on engineering problems for the department. Marion Whaley in whose deparment the computer was installed is quoted as saying:"This machine will shake engineers loose from the calculators and slide rules and allow them to get out on the jobs."

The following is a list of the organizations that installed the first computers that make up the early history in Boise. Each organization has stories about what applications were developed and the people that used them as seen through the eyes of the Idaho Statesman.
