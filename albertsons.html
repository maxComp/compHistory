Albertsons


The NCR 315 Data Processing System, introduced in January 1962, was a second-generation computer. All printed circuit boards used resistor-transistor logic to create the various logic elements. Basic memory was 5k of hand-made core memory, which was expandable to a maximum of 40k. Some devices like magnetic tape and the CRAM were daisy-chained to allow multiple drives to be connected.

The computer was located on the second floor of their office on 16th and State.
